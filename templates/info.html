NIH PTSD MISDIAGNOSIS STUDY

NIH PTSD UNDETECTED STUDY

Often, PTSD gets misdiagnosed as depression, anxiety, obsessiveness, and other mental health conditions causing mistreatment.

Most times, PTSD can go undetected due to factors of misconception, negligence, and an overwhelmingly unfair stigma

NCSR HARVARD SURVEY DATA

Path to Peace used interpreted data from a mental health survey that ioncluded PTSD to find the total percentile of adults with PTSD categorized via Sex and Age

After transferring the data into a database, we use Linear Regression Machine Learning Model in order to predict accuracy of users with PTSD

PCL-5 DOCUMENTATION

Path to Peace follows the PCL-5 scoring system used by the United State's government and liscenced practioners to diagnose individuals with PTSD

This 20 quesiton survey is judged off 31-33 point cut off in order to categorize individuals with PTSD
